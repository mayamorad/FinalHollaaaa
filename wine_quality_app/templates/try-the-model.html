Enter the properties of the wine to predict its quality based on the trained model.

Input Property Values

Fixed Acidity

Tartaric acid — most acids in wine are fixed (do not evaporate readily).

Volatile Acidity

The amount of acetic acid in the wine, which at higher levels can lead to an unpleasant, vinegar-like taste.

Citric Acid

Found in small quantities; citric acid can add 'freshness' and flavor to wines.

Residual Sugar

The amount of sugar remaining after fermentation stops. Wines with greater than 45g/L are considered sweet.

Chlorides

Sodium chloride (g/dm3). The amount of salt in the wine.

Free Sulfur

The free form of SO2. It prevents microbial growth and oxidation of wine.

Total Sulfur Dioxide

Free and bound SO2. In concentrations over 50 ppm, it becomes noticeable in the aroma and taste of wine.

Density

The density of wine, which depends on alcohol and sugar content, is close to that of water.

pH

Describes the acidity or alkalinity of the wine. Most wines fall between 3-4 on the pH scale.

Sulfates

A wine additive that can contribute to sulfur dioxide (SO2) levels, acting as an antimicrobial and antioxidant.

Alcohol

The percent alcohol content in the wine.
